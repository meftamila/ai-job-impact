Feature Engineering

Key design decisions in the preprocessing stage

Experience Level Grouping

Years of experience binned into Junior (1–10), Mid-level (11–20), Senior (21–30) for better categorical signal and model interpretability.

Data Leakage Prevention

Automation_Probability_2030 excluded from features — it directly encodes the target, which would produce misleadingly high accuracy scores.

Dual Binary Classification

Two separate XGBoost models predict Risk_Category_Low and Risk_Category_Medium independently, enabling nuanced per-class analysis.

One-Hot Encoding

Categorical variables (Job_Title, Education_Level, Experience group, Risk_Category) encoded with get_dummies, drop_first=False to retain full category info.

StandardScaler

Continuous features (salary, AI exposure index, 10 skill scores, years experience) normalized to zero mean and unit variance per model.

Stratified Splitting

Train-test split uses stratify parameter per target variable to maintain class proportions in both training and test sets (random_state=42).

About This Project

Workforce automation risk forecasting with ML

What does this model predict?

Given a worker's job title, education, experience, salary, and skill profile — the model classifies whether they fall into a Low or Medium automation risk category by 2030.

  • Uses AI Exposure Index and Tech Growth Factor as key signals
  • 10 skill dimensions capture breadth of worker capability
  • Separate models per risk class avoids class imbalance issues
  • Leakage-free: automation probability excluded from features
  • Stratified splits ensure representative evaluation
